Report Design Language
💅
What Mia expects from a $9.99 report
"If it looks like a spreadsheet or a textbook, I'm not reading it. If it looks like something from a wellness magazine I'd save on Pinterest — I'm reading every word, highlighting sections, and sending screenshots to my friends."
The Core Principle — It's a Book About Her

The report is NOT a data dump with chart wheels and aspect tables. It's a beautiful, personal narrative about who she is. Think: a letter from a wise friend, laid out like a premium wellness magazine, with the production quality of a coffee table book. She should feel like she's holding something valuable — not reading a generated document.

Format Specs

Delivery: Web page (primary) + downloadable PDF
Web: Beautiful scrollable page at celestia.com/report/mia-xxx
PDF: A4 portrait, 16-24 pages depending on report
Margins: Generous — 30mm+ sides. White space is luxury.
Reading time: 12-18 minutes (she reads it in one sitting in bed)

Feel & Vibe

Feels like: A letter from your wisest friend
Looks like: Kinfolk magazine meets therapy journal
NOT like: A technical chart readout or generic horoscope
Screenshottable: Every single page should be beautiful enough to post on Instagram Stories
Re-readable: She'll come back to this 3-4 times over months

Tone of Voice — How the Report Talks to Mia
❌ Never write like this
"Your natal Moon in Scorpio in the 4th House indicates a deep emotional nature with transformative tendencies in the domestic sphere. This placement suggests intensity in familial bonds and a propensity for psychological depth in matters relating to home and security."

Jargon. Cold. Sounds like a textbook. Mia stops reading after one paragraph.

✅ Always write like this
"You feel things at a depth most people can't handle. Including you, sometimes. Your Scorpio Moon means your emotions aren't surface-level — they're oceanic. When you love, you love with your entire chest. When you hurt, it's not a scratch — it's an earthquake. And here's the pattern you've probably noticed: the people closest to you get the most intense version of you."

Personal. Warm. Conversational. She reads this and thinks "how does it KNOW?"

Voice Rules

→ Second person always ("you," never "the native")
→ Present tense ("you feel" not "you may feel")
→ Specific, not hedge-y ("this is why" not "this might suggest")
→ Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)

Words We Use

→ Patterns, clarity, insight
→ "Here's why you do this"
→ "The thing you've always felt but couldn't name"
→ "You've probably noticed"

Words We Never Use

→ Destiny, fate, prophecy
→ "The native," "the querent"
→ "May suggest," "could indicate"
→ "Cosmic energy," "vibrations"
Page Layout Anatomy — Every Report Page

Every Page Has:

→ Placement badge at top (e.g. "☽ Moon in Scorpio · 4th House")
→ Section heading in Playfair Display serif
→ 2-3 short paragraphs of personal narrative
→ One "highlight box" — the key insight, terracotta left border
→ White space — at least 30% of each page is empty. Breathe.
→ Page number bottom center, subtle

Every Page Does NOT Have:

→ ❌ Chart wheels or aspect grids (save for appendix)
→ ❌ Walls of text (if a paragraph is >4 lines, split it)
→ ❌ Multiple placements on one page (one per page)
→ ❌ Ads, upsells, or CTAs mid-report
→ ❌ Generic filler text ("Virgo is an earth sign...")
→ ❌ Disclaimers or hedging every other sentence

Content Rules Per Section

Each Placement Section (1-2 pages):

→ Placement badge at top of page
→ Evocative title ("The Depth You Carry," not "Moon in Scorpio")
→ 2-3 paragraphs of personal narrative (200-300 words)
→ One highlight box — the pattern she'll recognize
→ Zero jargon in the body. Placement name only in the badge.
→ At least one sentence that makes her think "how does it KNOW?"

The "How Does It KNOW" Formula:

→ Start with something she already knows about herself but has never heard said out loud
→ Then name the pattern she's been living but couldn't articulate
→ Then give her the WHY — the chart explanation
→ End with something forward-looking — what she can do with this knowledge

This formula is the entire product. If the writing doesn't follow it, the report feels generic. If it does, she screams "HOW DOES IT KNOW" and screenshots it.
