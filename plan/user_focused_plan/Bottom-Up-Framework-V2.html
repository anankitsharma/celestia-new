The Bottom-Up Framework

Don't start with the market. Start with one person. Build the product for her. Grow through her. A reusable system for building products people can't stop talking about.

🎯
Start with ONE
Not a segment. Not a demographic. One real human with a name, a life, and a problem.
🔨
Build for HER
Every screen, every word, every notification, every price point — traced back to her specific reality.
📈
Grow through HER
She loves it so much she can't stop sharing. Growth is a side effect of delight, not a strategy.
❌ Top-Down
"The Lego resale market is $1.2B" → Build for "collectors ages 25-45" → Generic features for a vague crowd → Blast ads → Hope → 4% conversion, 20% churn
✅ Bottom-Up
"His name is Marcus. He buys Lego at Target every Saturday with his daughter" → Build the scanner HE needs → He shows it to 3 dads at the Lego meetup → They tell their friends → $0 CAC, 8% churn

WHO — Full name, demographics, financial reality

Phase 2
Build Every Pixel for Her
The product isn't a collection of features. It's a sequence of moments designed for ONE person. Every screen, every word, every animation answers: "what does she need right now?"
Screen-by-Screen Design from Her Perspective
Screen 1
👋
Onboarding
Collect ONLY what you need. Make every field explain WHY it matters to HER. Show instant value within 30 seconds of opening.
Screen 2
⭐
The "Wow" Moment
Within 60 seconds she should think "how does it know?" or "this is exactly what I needed." This screen determines if she stays or deletes.
Screen 3
🏠
Home / Daily
What she sees every time she opens the app. Personalized. Fresh. Never the same twice. This is the habit surface.
Screen 4
🔍
Core Action
The ONE thing she came to do. Scan a Lego. Check compatibility. Log a workout. Zero friction. One tap to start.
Screen 5
📊
The Result
Beautiful. Personal. Screenshottable. Includes a share button. This is simultaneously the product AND the marketing asset.
Screen 6
🔗
The Bridge
After the result: "Go deeper?" or "Share this?" or "Compare with someone?" Every result leads somewhere. No dead ends.

Product Design Decisions — All Traced to Her
Voice & Tone → Use HER words. Interview real users and steal their exact phrases.
Feature Priority → Build the ONE thing she does most, first. Everything else is Phase 2+.
Design Language → Match HER aesthetic world. If she lives on Pinterest, make it Pinterest-worthy.
Navigation → Bridge every screen. Never let her think "where do I go now?"
Pricing → What can she afford without thinking? $0.23/day ≠ $6.99/mo in her head.
Free vs Paid → Free must be genuinely great. Paid unlocks at moments of peak curiosity, not random pop-ups.
Notifications → Every push must be a gift she'd miss if it stopped. Not a reminder. Not a nag.
Sharing → Built into every result BY DESIGN. Not a "share button" added later.

03
Phase 3
Build the Daily Habit
She downloaded. Now she needs a reason to open tomorrow. And the day after. And 6 months from now. The habit system is what separates apps that last from apps that get deleted.
🌅
Morning Trigger
A push notification that's worth reading before she gets out of bed. Personalized. Fresh daily. Timed to HER alarm.
📅
Weekly Anchor
One deeper piece of content per week (report, recap, plan). She plans her week around it. Premium conversion trigger.
🧠
Memory Layer
The product remembers her history. Gets smarter over time. Switching to a competitor = starting from zero. Ethical lock-in.
🏆
Progress Identity
She sees herself in the data. Her collection value. Her streak. Her chart. The product becomes part of how she defines herself.

04
Phase 4
Monetize at Peak Curiosity
Never sell when she's calm. Sell when she NEEDS the answer and the free version got her 80% there. The paywall should feel like a door she wants to open, not a wall blocking her.
🆓
Free = Genuinely Great
Good enough to keep using forever. She tells friends about the FREE version. It's not broken or limited — it's focused. The upgrade adds depth, not access.
😳
Trigger = Emotional Peak
She just scanned a rare set and the full value history is blurred. She just read "The Spark" and "Where You'll Fight" is locked. She WANTS the answer. Now.
✨
Upgrade = Natural Step
"Of course I want this." It shouldn't feel like buying. It should feel like unlocking something that was always hers. The next room in her house.
Pricing Decisions Traced to Her
Monthly price = What she spends without thinking. Her daily coffee cost ÷ 30 is your ceiling.
Trial length = How long she needs to experience the anchor value. If the weekly report converts, 7-day trial (she must see one Monday).
One-time purchases = For users who won't subscribe but WILL impulse-buy at the moment of peak curiosity.
Annual offer = After 2+ months subscribed. "Save 40%." Offered when she's proven she won't churn.
The blur mechanic = Show WHAT's behind the paywall. Visible titles, blurred content. Curiosity is the conversion.
Never at rest = No random "upgrade now!" pop-ups. Only at moments of peak emotional engagement.

05
Phase 5
Go Where She Already Lives
Map her digital world. Each platform has ONE job. Your content should be indistinguishable from content made BY users of that platform — not content made FOR users of that platform.
HER PLATFORM MAP
Discovery platform (TikTok / YouTube Shorts / Reddit) → She FINDS you here. Reach strangers. Hook in 2 seconds.
Engagement platform (Instagram / Twitter / Discord) → She FOLLOWS you here. Saves, shares, DMs. Deepens the relationship.
Research platform (Pinterest / Google / YouTube) → She SEARCHES here. Long-tail. Evergreen. High intent.
Conversion surface (Website / App Store) → She ACTS here. Enters data. Downloads. Pays.
Retention channels (Push / Email / In-App) → She RETURNS here. Daily habits. Weekly anchors.
EACH PLATFORM = ONE JOB
Don't ask every platform to do everything. TikTok doesn't convert — it creates awareness. Pinterest doesn't go viral — it compounds traffic for 90 days. Email doesn't acquire — it retains and sells. Match the platform to its natural strength in HER specific behavior pattern.
THE VOICE TEST
"Would she say this to a friend?" If yes → publish. If it sounds like a brand wrote it → rewrite. The best marketing is indistinguishable from organic conversation between users.

06
Phase 6
Let Her Taste Before She Commits
"Download our app" is a huge ask from a stranger. Give her a taste of the product ON THE WEB first. Once she's had the "wow" moment, the download isn't a leap of faith — it's an inevitable next step.
🌐
Free Web Tool
One interactive tool that delivers instant value. She enters data → gets a result → has the "wow" moment. No download required. Birthday = conversion point.
💬
Web Chat / Demo (3-4 interactions)
Let her TALK to the product. Enough to prove it works. Not enough to satisfy. She hits the limit at peak curiosity → downloads to continue.
📄
Free Mini-Result
A beautiful, screenshottable preview of her personalized result. She shares it → friends see it → they enter their data → the loop begins.
The Seamless Handoff — Web → App
Rule 1: Transfer ALL data automatically. If she re-enters anything in the app, you've failed.
Rule 2: The app greets her by name with context. "Welcome, Marcus — your Millennium Falcon scan is saved."
Rule 3: It should feel like walking from one room to the next in the same house. Not like starting over in a new product.

07
Phase 7
Let Her Scale It
Don't build a referral program. Build a product so specific and so remarkable that she shares it because she CAN'T NOT. Then build sharing into every core action so the growth is automatic.
📸
Screenshot Trigger
Every result is designed to be screenshotted and shared. Your branding is visible. Every screenshot = a micro-billboard.
👥
Two-Person Action
At least one core feature naturally involves a second person. Compatibility. Comparison. Competition. Each action = 1 new user acquired for $0.
💬
Group Chat Contagion
She shares to her 4-person group chat. 2 download. Each shares to THEIR group chat. Exponential. The group chat is your growth team.

The Litmus Test
7 Questions for Every Decision
Before building any feature, writing any copy, choosing any price, sending any notification — ask:
1. Can I picture [Name] using this at a specific moment in her day?
2. Would [Name] use THESE exact words to describe this to a friend?
3. Would [Name] screenshot this and send it to someone?
4. Does this feature naturally involve or invite another person?
5. Would [Name] miss this if we removed it tomorrow?
6. Does this feel like a gift or an interruption?
7. Would [Name] pay for this — at THIS price — at the moment she'd encounter it?

5+ YES answers = build it. 0 YES answers = go back to Phase 1 and re-read her internal monologue.
