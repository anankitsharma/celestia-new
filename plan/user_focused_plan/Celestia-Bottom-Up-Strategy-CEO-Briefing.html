The Bottom-Up Approach

How we plan everything — product, marketing, content, growth — starting from one single person, not from a market size spreadsheet

Written for a CEO who doesn't do marketing jargon
There are two ways to build a company

❌ Top-Down

Start with the market. Work down to the person.

  • 📊"The astrology market is $12.8 billion"
  • 🎯"Our target: women 18-34 interested in astrology"
  • 📢"Let's run ads to 'astrology enthusiasts'"
  • 🤞"Let's hope someone converts"
  • 💸Burns $50K on ads before finding product-market fit
  • 😐10,000 people kinda like you. None love you.

This is how most startups waste their first year.

vs

✅ Bottom-Up

Start with ONE person. Build up from her.

  • 👤"Her name is Mia. She's 24. She lives in Austin."
  • 💔"She Googles 'virgo scorpio compatibility' at 10:30pm"
  • ✨"We build the page that answers HER question"
  • 🤯"She tries it. Her eyes go wide. She screenshots it."
  • 💬"She sends it to 4 friends. They all sign up."
  • 🔥100 people who LOVE you. They bring 300 more.

This is how Co-Star got to 20M downloads.

"It's better to have 100 users who love you than 10,000 who sort of like you."

— Paul Graham, Y Combinator founder

The bottom-up approach means: every single decision — what feature to build, what content to post, which platform to focus on, what price to charge — is answered by asking one question:

"What would Mia do?"

How it works, step by step

From One Person to an Entire Business

Every step flows from the one before it. Nothing is guessed.

Step 1 — Start With ONE Person
We don't say "women 18-34." We say: "Her name is Mia Reyes."
She's 24. Lives in Austin, TX (SoCo neighborhood, 78704). Marketing coordinator. $44K/year. Virgo Sun, Scorpio Moon. Single. iPhone 14 in her hand at 10:30pm. We know what's on her nightstand. We know her exact address. This isn't a demographic — this is a real person we can picture.

Step 2 — Understand Her Pain
WHY does she need Celestia? What's the real problem?
She keeps dating the same type. She can't afford therapy. Co-Star feels cold. She feels behind in life. She doesn't want entertainment — she wants to understand WHY things keep happening to her. The pain isn't "I need an astrology app." The pain is "I need to understand myself and no one is helping me."
Step 3 — Build What She Needs
The product is shaped by her pain, not by what competitors do.
She needs warm, personal, specific answers — not cold data. So we build an AI that talks like a best friend, not a textbook. She can't afford $200 therapy — so we price reports at $9.99. She wants to understand patterns — so we show her Venus placement, not just her sun sign. Every feature is a direct response to a specific pain she has.

Step 4 — Go Where She Already Is
We don't pick channels randomly. We follow Mia.
She scrolls TikTok 2+ hrs/day → so we make TikTok reels. She Googles at 10:30pm → so we build SEO pages. She saves Instagram carousels → so we make shareable zodiac content. She sends screenshots to group chats → so we build a viral sharing feature. We're not "doing marketing." We're showing up where she already spends her time.
Step 5 — Speak Her Language
The content isn't what WE want to say. It's what SHE needs to hear.
She doesn't search "natal chart interpretation." She searches "why do I keep attracting unavailable men." So our content answers THAT question — in her words, not astrology jargon. Every TikTok hook, every SEO page title, every Instagram caption is written in the exact language she uses when she's lying in bed at 10:30pm trying to understand her life.

She pays because the free value already proved we understand her.
We give away the birth chart for free. She's already amazed. The AI chat gives her one insight that makes her eyes go wide. NOW she trusts us. The $9.99 report isn't a sales pitch — it's a natural "I need to know more." She pays less than a Starbucks order. She doesn't think twice.

Mia brings 3-5 friends. Each friend brings 2-3 more. Growth becomes free.

How every business decision traces back to Mia

The "Ask Mia" Decision Framework

Every question the CEO asks has an answer that comes from knowing Mia.

CEO asks: "What should our app look like?"
Warm, earthy, Desert Dawn palette. NOT dark mode.
Because Mia shops at Free People, saves boho Pinterest boards, and wants warmth — not a NASA terminal. Every competitor is dark mode. We own warmth.
CEO asks: "What price should we charge?"
$9.99 per report. Not $29. Not $4.99.
Because Mia makes $44K/yr. $29 feels like a commitment. $4.99 feels cheap. $9.99 is "less than my Starbucks today." She pays without thinking twice.
CEO asks: "Which platform do we focus on first?"
TikTok for awareness. Google SEO for conversion.
Because Mia scrolls TikTok 2+ hrs/day (that's where she discovers). But she Googles with emotional urgency at 10:30pm (that's where she converts and pays).
CEO asks: "What should our AI sound like?"
A warm, honest best friend. Not a guru.
Because Mia is turned off by "mystical guru" energy. She wants someone who talks like her smart friend who happens to know astrology deeply. Direct. Specific. No sugarcoating.
CEO asks: "What content do we post?"
"POV: you're a Virgo and you wonder why every relationship ends the same way"
Because Mia doesn't search "natal chart aspects." She searches "why do I keep attracting the same type." Our content uses HER words, not astrology textbook words.
CEO asks: "What feature do we build next?"
Compatibility checker with shareable links.
Because Mia just met a guy and the first thing she wants to know is "are we compatible?" And when she shares the link, the guy enters HIS birth data too — that's a new user for free.
