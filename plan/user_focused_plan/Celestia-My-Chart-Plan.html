Three Layers of the Chart Experience

👆

My Patterns — The "Why You Do That Thing" Section

Aspects are the most powerful — and most confusing — part of astrology. We DON'T call them "aspects." We call them "Patterns." Because that's what they are: the recurring themes in her life that she can't explain. We also DON'T use geometry language ("trine," "square"). We use human language ("flow," "tension," "power").

We Rename Everything — No Jargon, Just Life
Astro jargon → Celestia language:
Trine → Flow (these parts of you work together easily)
Sextile → Boost (natural talent, slight effort unlocks it)
Conjunction → Fusion (these energies are merged — amplified)
Square → Tension (these parts of you fight each other)
Opposition → Tug of War (pull between two extremes)
Color coding:
🟢 Green — Flow / Boost (easy, natural)
🟡 Gold — Fusion (powerful, amplified)
🔴 Rose — Tension (challenging, growth-edge)
🟠 Terra — Tug of War (polarizing, dynamic)

She never needs to learn what a "square" is. She just sees "Tension: Venus ↔ Neptune" and reads why she idealizes partners.
Tension
Venus ↔ Neptune
Why You Fall in Love With Potential, Not Reality
This is probably the pattern that's cost you the most heartbreak. Your Venus (how you love) is locked in a tension with Neptune (the planet of illusion and idealization). What this means in practice: you don't fall for who someone IS. You fall for who they COULD be. You see the best possible version of someone and then love THAT person — while the real person standing in front of you is waving red flags you genuinely cannot see. This isn't naivety. It's a wiring in your chart. The good news: now that you can name it, you can start catching it in real time.
Flow
Sun ↔ Jupiter
The Quiet Confidence That Saves You Every Time
Even when everything feels like it's falling apart, there's a part of you that quietly believes it'll work out. That's your Sun flowing with Jupiter — the planet of expansion and luck. This aspect gives you a resilience that other people genuinely envy. You bounce back. Not quickly, not dramatically, but inevitably. People call it "positivity" but it's deeper than that — it's a bone-deep trust that the story isn't over yet.
Tug of War
Moon ↔ Mars
The Battle Between Feeling and Doing
You feel things deeply (Scorpio Moon), but when it comes to acting on those feelings, something blocks you (Mars in the 12th house). The result: you swallow your anger until it leaks out sideways — passive-aggressive comments, silent treatment, or eventually an explosion that seems "out of nowhere" to everyone else but has actually been building for weeks. This opposition is asking you to learn that anger isn't dangerous. It's information.

Depth Levels — One Chart, Two Audiences
The Toggle: "Simple" vs "Detailed"

Mia is the default user. She sees warm, personal language with zero jargon. But her friend who's been studying astrology for 3 years wants to see degrees, exact orbs, and house system options. Both are served by ONE chart with a depth toggle.

✦ Simple — Default
⚙️ Detailed — For Nerds

✦ Simple Mode (Default for Mia)

→ Planet names in English ("Your Venus" not "♀")
→ Signs written out ("Scorpio" not "♏")
→ Houses as rooms ("Your Mirror" not "7th House")
→ Aspects as patterns ("Tension" not "Square")
→ No degrees, no orbs, no technical notation
→ Warm personal text for everything
→ She never needs to know what "14° Virgo" means

⚙️ Detailed Mode (For astro-nerds)

→ Exact degrees ("Venus at 14°32' Virgo")
→ Aspect orbs ("Venus square Neptune, orb 2°14'")
→ Traditional aspect names shown alongside Celestia names
→ House system selector (Placidus, Whole Sign, Porphyry)
→ Full aspect table grid
→ Planetary dignities (domicile, detriment, exaltation, fall)
→ Same warm text PLUS technical addendum

The default is ALWAYS Simple. The toggle is tucked in a corner — visible but not prominent. 90% of users never touch it. 10% love that it exists. Both feel served.

Non-Negotiable Rules for My Chart
Always
✓ Beautiful before informative — if it's ugly, she'll never explore
✓ Tappable EVERYTHING — no dead elements on the chart
✓ Big 3 expanded by default — she doesn't have to figure out what to tap first
✓ Friendly names for everything — "Your Mirror" not "7th House"
✓ One "how does it KNOW" moment per planet
✓ Every section has "Ask Celestia more →" — seamless bridge to chat
✓ Chart fits on one phone screen — no scrolling to see the wheel
✓ Simple mode is ALWAYS the default
✓ 4 sharing options visible — screenshot, link, card, compare
Never
✗ Show a wall of symbols on first load — she'll close the app
✗ Use technical jargon by default — "trine," "orb," "domicile" mean nothing to her
✗ Show an aspect grid/table as the main view — that's a spreadsheet
✗ Make the wheel tiny and unreadable — it should be the hero visual
✗ Auto-show all 12 planets at once — start with 6, let her discover
✗ Have dead/non-tappable elements — everything that looks tappable must BE tappable
✗ Make Detailed mode the default — this kills 90% of engagement
✗ Forget the share buttons — the chart is the viral asset, sharing must be frictionless
