# 2D Card Navigation — How Mia Actually Reads This

**Status:** supersedes the vertical-reel model in `05-today-concept.md`. Same 4–6 card lineup, same migration table — only the navigation grammar changes.

## What changed and why

`05` proposed pure-vertical advancement (TikTok/Reels gesture). That was wrong for **reading** astrology content. Three problems:

1. **Vertical-swipe-to-advance conflicts with vertical-scroll-to-read.** If Mia wants to read more text on the current card, the gesture system has no way to tell "scroll for content" apart from "advance to next card." Either content is forbidden (Tiktok-style — too sparse) or the gestures fight (Mia overshoots cards).
2. **Reading is a vertical activity.** Every text-heavy app on her phone (Apple News, Substack, IG carousels, web pages) uses vertical scroll for "more of this thing." Forcing her to tap-to-deepen on a separate sheet breaks the expected pattern.
3. **One-handed thumb position.** At 10:30pm in bed, Mia's phone is in her right hand, thumb resting near the bottom-right. Horizontal swipes from the bottom-right are natural; vertical-swipe-from-mid-screen is awkward.

## The new grammar — two axes, two meanings

```
                    ↑ scroll up
                       │
                       │
   ← swipe left ───── CARD ───── swipe right →
   (next card)         │         (previous card)
                       │
                    ↓ scroll down
                  (more depth)
```

| Axis | Meaning | Mental model |
|---|---|---|
| **Horizontal** (swipe left/right) | Move between today's cards | "Next page in a magazine" — Apple News, Snapchat Discover, IG Stories, App Store |
| **Vertical** (scroll up/down) | Read more about *this* card | "Web page scroll" — universal. Every reading app. |

**Key insight:** vertical = depth, horizontal = breadth. Reading more about today's anchor doesn't take Mia to a new screen, modal, or tab — it just keeps scrolling within Card 1. When she's ready to move on, she swipes left.

## Mia's Sunday-night reading (10:30pm walkthrough)

This is the experience scenario. If this feels right, the design is right.

### Beat 1 — Open

Mia is in bed, light off, phone unlocked. She taps the Celestia icon. The app opens directly to Card 1 (Anchor). No splash, no menu, no choice — the content is already there.

The first thing her eyes register: a single Playfair Display sentence on a warm gradient.

> *"Today wants you slower."*

Below it, in DM Sans:

> *"Mars in your 7th asks you to listen first."*

Top-right: a small ✦ Celestia mark and her avatar with a quiet "🔥 12" beside it. Bottom-right: a share icon, half-faded. Top edge: 6 small dots, the first one filled.

She has read 11 words and seen the brand. Total time: 1.5 seconds. Already screenshot-worthy.

### Beat 2 — Decision point (~3 seconds in)

Three paths fork from here. She picks one.

**Path A — Share immediately.** The sentence hit her. She taps the share icon → iOS share sheet → "iMessage" → group chat with Sara and Chloe → "look at this" → send. Total: 6 seconds. App goes to background. **Job done. Mia is satisfied.**

**Path B — Chew on it.** She scrolls down on Card 1.

The card extends. Below the headline, a fuller reading appears:

> *"Mars moved into your 7th house this morning. The 7th is your one-on-one space — partners, close collaborations, the people who make you most yourself. Mars there means action, but also conflict. The next 4 days will surface things that have been quietly resolving themselves. Don't push. Let people show you what they want to do."*
>
> *"You'll want to react. Don't."*

Below that, a quiet input:

> *"Ask Celestia about today"* — with a placeholder cursor blinking.

Mia reads the paragraphs. Doesn't type anything. Stays for 30 seconds.

**Path C — Skip ahead.** The headline didn't hit her tonight. She swipes left. Card 1 slides off, Card 2 slides in.

### Beat 3 — Card 2 (Spotlight)

Same pattern. Top: a colored tag — `LOVE` in subtle terracotta. Headline:

> *"Today wants honesty over comfort."*

Subline:

> *"Venus is moving through your 5th house. There's one conversation you've been rehearsing in the shower."*

Mia scrolls down. The full reading appears: 2 paragraphs about why this is happening, then a soft prompt: *"What's the conversation?"* with chat input.

She types: *"i think i need to tell jake we're not actually friends anymore"*. Hits send. Below, an AI response unfurls — 3 sentences specific to her chart, calling out her Scorpio Moon's avoidance of confrontation and her Leo Rising's actual comfort with hard truths once they're in motion.

She stops. Reads twice. **This is the moment Co-Star never gave her.** She screenshots the AI response, sends to her best friend, captions it: "WHAT."

She doesn't scroll back up. She swipes left to Card 3.

### Beat 4 — Card 3 (Sky)

> *"The Moon enters Cancer at 2:14am tonight."*
>
> *"Your dream-sleep zone gets emotional weight tonight. Pay attention to what surfaces."*

She scrolls down. Sees a small visual — a moon glyph moving through a tiny chart preview, today's transit highlighted. Reads one paragraph. Doesn't engage further.

Swipes left.

### Beat 5 — Card 4 (Reflect)

Different shape. Top:

> *"How did today actually feel?"*

Below it: a chat-style input, but pre-warmed with a smaller suggestion: *"Or just say one word."*

She types: *"weird."* Hits send.

The AI responds: *"What kind of weird?"*

She types: *"like everyone was being normal but i was watching myself from outside"*

The AI responds with something specific — references her Scorpio Moon, the day's Mercury aspect, asks a follow-up that lands.

She talks for 4 minutes. Eventually tapers off. Locks the phone halfway through typing a thought. *(Comes back tomorrow. Conversation is preserved.)*

### Beat 6 — Card 5 (Trigger)

She comes back later that night. Re-opens the app. Lands on Card 1 again. Swipes left, left, left, left — gets to Card 5 because it's Sunday and the Sunday Week Reflection is firing.

> *"You're 12 days into Mars in Aries. How are you holding up?"*

A small visual: 5 dots representing the past 5 Sundays. Tap-deepen scrolls to a structured "what changed this week" prompt with last week's saved insights re-surfaced.

This is the trigger card — only fires when there's a meaningful weekly/monthly/event-based moment. Most days, this slot doesn't render and the reel is just 5 cards.

### Beat 7 — Card 6 (Closing)

> *"That's today."*
>
> *"The sky shifts again at 6am. Sleep well, Mia."*

Below: a single shareable summary card showing the day's anchor + spotlight as a single clean image. Below that: small text links — *ask · chart · journal*. Bottom-right: "🔥 13" — her streak quietly ticked up.

She doesn't tap anything. She closes the app. Phone goes dark. **Session complete.**

Total time spent: **6 minutes 40 seconds** including the 4-minute chat. Some nights it's 90 seconds. Both are wins.

## What this gives Mia that the current Today doesn't

1. **One thing at a time.** Her cognitive load per "screen" is one card, not 24.
2. **Reading is reading.** Scroll down for more about this thought, not a context-breaking modal.
3. **Self-paced depth.** She decides per-card: skim, share, or dig in.
4. **A clear end.** Card 6 says "that's today." She knows the experience is complete and can close at peace.
5. **Screenshot artifact at every level.** Headline alone is a screenshot; full card is a screenshot; AI response within a card is a screenshot.
6. **Conversation in context.** The AI chat is *about this card*, not a separate destination. Today's content scaffolds the chat's specificity.

## What this gives the team

1. **No new component for "deepen modal" or "half-sheet."** Depth is just longer card content. The card is the deepening surface.
2. **Reuse existing deep-dive content.** The current `LIFE AREA DEEP DIVE MODAL` content (12 sections per area) becomes the scrollable lower portion of Card 2. Drop the modal, keep the structure.
3. **Easier share-card composition.** Each card is already a self-contained vertical strip; render-to-image is straightforward (existing `*ShareCard.js` patterns).
4. **AI chat reuses existing hooks.** No new modal scaffolding — it's an inline section at the bottom of relevant cards, using the existing `generateChatResponse` and session management.

```
       │
       │  ←─── swipe left to advance horizontally ───→
       │
       ▼

PER-CARD (vertical content):

┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│ ●─●─○─○─○─○                      │  ← progress dots

│ ─── scroll down for more ─────── │  ← affordance hint (optional)
│                                  │
│                                  │     ↓ vertical scroll within card
│   THE READING                    │
│                                  │
│   Mars moved into your 7th       │
│   house this morning. The 7th    │  ← longer text content
│   is your one-on-one space —     │
│   partners, close collaborations,│
│   the people who make you most   │
│   yourself.                      │
│                                  │
│   You'll want to react. Don't.   │
│                                  │
│   ─────────────────────────      │  ← divider
│                                  │
│   Ask Celestia about today       │  ← chat input
│   ┌──────────────────────┐       │
│   │  Type your thought...│       │
│   └──────────────────────┘       │

```

**Above the fold**: just the headline + subline + brand + share. The single screenshot artifact.

**Below the fold**: the reading paragraphs, then the chat input. Mia can stay there as long as she wants. The card has no inherent length cap — it just ends when the content does.

**Horizontal swipe at any vertical scroll position**: takes her to the next card. The next card opens at *its* top, regardless of where she was in the previous one.

## Per-card content shape

### Card 1 — ANCHOR
- **Above fold:** headline + subline + brand + share
- **Below fold:** full reading (3–4 paragraphs from `forecast.navigatorSummary` + `navigateToward` content rephrased as flowing prose, not bullets), then chat input *"Ask about today."*
- **Bottom of card:** small footer line — *"Today's energy is set. Sky updates again at 6am."*

### Card 2 — SPOTLIGHT (top life area)
- **Above fold:** life area tag + headline + 1-sentence read
- **Below fold:** the existing 12-section deep dive content (planetary reason, full horoscope, navigate toward, navigate around, timing, ritual, affirmation, navigator note) — flowing scroll, not modal
- **Bottom:** *"See all 5 life areas →"* — small link, opens a stacked view of the other 4 (could be a side-modal or another tab, low-frequency)

### Card 3 — SKY (top transit)
- **Above fold:** transit headline + subline
- **Below fold:** longer transit reading + a small visual chart preview showing the active aspect, then optional "see other transits today" link
- **Bottom:** quiet text link to full chart

### Card 4 — REFLECT (chat-native)
- **Above fold:** time-of-day prompt question (different morning/afternoon/evening/late-night)
- **Below fold:** chat input + auto-grow conversation. THIS card IS the chat experience for Mia, in the context of today.
- **No share button** on this card — chats are private by default. Share is per-message via long-press if she wants.

### Card 5 — TRIGGER (conditional, max 1)
- **Above fold:** trigger headline (e.g. *"This week's pattern"*, *"Solar return in 4 days"*, *"Mercury starts retrograde tonight"*)
- **Below fold:** trigger-specific content
- **Bottom:** trigger-specific CTA (e.g. *"See your year ahead →"* for birthday week)

### Card 6 — CLOSING
- **Above fold:** *"That's today."* + closing line + tiny streak indicator
- **Below fold:** the day's shareable recap card (rendered image, ready to save/share), then quiet text links *ask · chart · journal*
- **Bottom:** nothing. This is the floor.

## Gestures — full grammar

| Gesture | Behavior |
|---|---|
| Swipe left | Next card (horizontal advance) |
| Swipe right | Previous card |
| Vertical scroll up/down | Read more/less of current card |
| Tap card content | Default tap behavior of the element (e.g., text doesn't react; chat input opens keyboard) |
| Tap share icon | iOS share sheet with pre-rendered card image |
| Double-tap anywhere on card | Save the card to her Journey/Saved (heart pulse) |
| Long-press on card | Reaction sheet: 🪞 *me* · 💭 *think* · 💔 *true* · ✦ *save* · 🚫 *less* |
| Pull-down at top of Card 1 | Refresh "now" data (sky transits, moon phase) |
| Tap a progress dot at top | Jump to that card |
| Swipe down from very top | Close to home (iOS native "drag to dismiss" — only if implemented as a modal stack on top of tabs) |

**Key resolution rule:** at the very top of a card (scroll position 0), horizontal swipes are unambiguous. Below that, horizontal swipes are still horizontal, but the gesture system needs ~15° angle tolerance — a mostly-horizontal drag advances the card, a mostly-vertical drag scrolls. iOS handles this well with `react-native-pager-view` + nested ScrollView. Standard pattern, no innovation needed.

## Why this is better than `05`'s vertical-only

| Aspect | `05` Vertical-only | `06` 2D navigation |
|---|---|---|
| Reading more text | Forced into "tap to deepen → modal" | Just scroll down. Universal pattern. |
| Cognitive model | TikTok-feed (consume) | Apple News magazine (read) |
| Gesture conflict | Vertical-swipe vs vertical-scroll fight | Each axis has one meaning, no conflict |
| One-handed thumb | Awkward for vertical swipe at chest level | Horizontal swipe near thumb rest = perfect |
| Reusing existing deep-dive content | Requires modal/sheet engineering | Drops in as scrollable card content. No new components. |
| Implied tone | Compulsive consumption | Considered reading |
| Mia's existing muscle memory | TikTok | Apple News + Snapchat Discover + IG Stories — all three at once |

## Open questions remaining

Most are answered. These need a check:

1. **Should horizontal swipe loop?** (after Card 6 → Card 1?) My instinct: **no**. Closing card is the floor. After Card 6 → Card 6 stays. She has to swipe right (back) to revisit anything.
2. **Should each card remember its scroll position?** (return to Card 1 after going to Card 4 → land back where she was?) My instinct: **yes**, but only within the same session. Coming back tomorrow always opens at Card 1 top.
3. **Should the chat on Card 4 persist day-to-day?** (her "today" chat from yesterday visible if she scrolls history?) My instinct: **no for v1** — Card 4 is a fresh prompt each day. Persistent chat history lives in the Ask AI tab.
4. **Are the progress dots at top tappable?** My instinct: **yes** — fast nav for repeat visitors. They get scoped to today's actual card count (5 if no trigger, 6 if trigger).

## What `05`'s migration table still says (unchanged)

The card-by-card content selection, slot assignments, and migration table from `05-today-concept.md` are **unchanged**. The only thing different is *how Mia moves between and within them*. Slots stay the same. Selector rule stays the same. Trigger priority stays the same.

What `05` called "tap to deepen → half-sheet AI chat" → now is just "scroll down on the same card to find chat input."

What `05` called "deep-dive modal for Spotlight" → now is just "scroll down on Card 2 to read the full life-area content."

The redesign got *simpler* by collapsing depth into vertical scroll instead of modal stacks.
