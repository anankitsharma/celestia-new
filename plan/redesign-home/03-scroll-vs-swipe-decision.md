# Scroll vs Swipe — Picking the Right Interaction Grammar

The user's question: should Today work like **TikTok/Reels** (vertical scroll, one card at a time) or like **Tinder/Hinge** (swipe-card stack)? Both are familiar to Mia. They are NOT equivalent.

## The two grammars are psychologically opposite

| Dimension | Vertical scroll (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) | Swipe cards (Tinder/Bumble) |
|---|---|---|
| User's role | **Passenger** — algorithm decides | **Judge** — user decides yes/no |
| Per-card cognition | Low: "watch and react" | High: "evaluate and swipe" |
| Decision burden | Near-zero — keep scrolling | Forced choice every card |
| Direction semantics | Up = next, down = back. Direction is just navigation. | Left = reject, right = keep. Direction is *meaning*. |
| Affective register | Dopamine baseline / passive entertainment | Self-as-judge / appraising / hot |
| Bounded? | Infinite feed | Finite deck (today's batch) |
| Output | Time spent | A "yes" pile and a "no" pile |
| What happens on bail | She closes the app, no consequence | Unprocessed cards remain — a kind of pending todo |

## What Mia is actually doing at 10:30pm

She's emotionally activated by a trigger event ("left on read", "Sunday anxiety", "new guy"). Her job is **not** "consume content" and **not** "make decisions." Her job is **"feel seen and optionally go deeper."**

Mapped against the two grammars:

- **Vertical scroll** matches "feel seen" — she encounters a sequence of moments, no judgment required, advances when ready, exits at peace.
- **Swipe cards** mismatches "feel seen" — swiping left on an astrology insight = "not me today" trains rejection of self-knowledge. Even if we relabel left as "save for later" instead of "no," the muscle memory from Tinder is "I am evaluating this thing." Wrong frame for introspection.

Hinge is closer to right than Tinder, because Hinge users don't actually swipe — they scroll vertically through a single profile and *tap* one specific prompt to engage. Hinge's atomic interaction is **choose-which-to-react-to**, not **judge-each-one**. That's the bit worth borrowing.

## The actual right pattern is hybrid

Borrow these specific moves:

| Borrow from | What | Why |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | **Vertical advancement, full-bleed cards, no chrome** | Matches her muscle memory; zero decision burden to advance |
| Hinge | **Tap-to-engage on a specific card, not all of them** | Mia chooses which moment to deepen; everything doesn't demand a reaction |
| IG Stories | **Tap-to-advance with progress dots at top** | Bounded — she sees "I'm 2 of 6 today" — vs. TikTok's never-ending tunnel |
| Apple News / Calm | **Finite "today" set, then a soft floor** | She can run out of today's cards naturally and feel done |
| Spotify Wrapped | **Each card is its own quotable artifact** | Screenshot-grade content, brand visible, one fact per card |

**Synthesis:** vertical scroll (or vertical tap-advance) of a **finite** set of full-bleed cards, with **tap to deepen** on whichever card resonates, and a clear **end** (Mia knows she's done with today). No left/right swipe meaning. No infinite feed. No judgment overlay.

## Why NOT pure TikTok-style infinite scroll

- Mia's lapsed Co-Star reason was "feels generic and meaningless" — infinite scroll *also* feels generic and meaningless because every card is interchangeable. Bounded > infinite for self-reflection.
- The "completion" affordance is critical for nighttime use. Mia needs to feel done, then close the app and sleep. An app that never says "you're done with today" trains compulsion, not closure.
- Astrology content depth isn't TikTok-content depth. A TikTok video is consumed once. An astrology insight Mia might want to return to. Saving/pinning matters.

## Why NOT pure Tinder/Bumble swipe deck

- Importing left-as-reject psychology contaminates the introspection frame.
- Forcing her to swipe each card is decision-burden she doesn't want at 10:30pm.
- The "deck of strangers" metaphor conflicts with "this is *your* day" intimacy.
- Hinge already broke from this pattern for the right reason — depth needs choice, not forced binary.

## The recommendation

Today is **a vertical, tap-or-scroll-advanced sequence of 4–6 full-bleed bounded cards**, each one screenshot-grade, with tap-to-deepen on cards that matter. Progress dots show position. After the last card, a soft "see you tomorrow" or "want to go deeper?" handoff to Chart / Chat / Reports.

Not Tinder. Not infinite TikTok. **A finite, vertical-advance, tap-to-deepen reel of today.**

## Open question that depends on this decision

If we go with this hybrid pattern, the open question becomes: **does the card advance via tap (IG Stories) or via vertical swipe (TikTok)?**

- **Tap-advance** (IG Stories): one-handed, thumb-on-right, never miss a card. But it conflicts with vertical scroll instinct on a tall iOS screen. Users sometimes scroll instead of tap.
- **Vertical swipe** (TikTok): muscle-memory match for the gesture. But it imports the "consume and forget" rhythm — she might swipe past a card that mattered without registering it.

My instinct: **vertical swipe** for advancement (she knows the gesture, it's natural at 10:30pm in bed), but each card has a **clear "deepen" tap target** (not the whole card — a specific element) that does NOT trigger on accidental swipe-tap. The deepen action is a deliberate finger-press, not a swipe consequence.

This needs UX validation but it's a safe starting point.
